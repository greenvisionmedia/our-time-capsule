Helping kids value their own voices and the voices of others.

We’re on a mission to build a generation of confident, connected, and empathetic kids.

There’s a lot going on in the world: the lingering impacts of pandemic, intense polarization, rise in everyday gun violence and the breakneck speed of social media and news.

What We Do

Once Upon Our Time Capsule reimagines the time capsule as a tool to helps kids document, share, and amplify their stories. Each June, we launch a new Youth Time Capsule, with a new theme, and support kids to contribute. We partner with organizations such as KIPP schools, Communities in Schools, the YMCA, and the Chicago Public Library to reach diverse youth where they live, learn and play.

Document

Through our structured curriculum, teacher support, and "time capsule kits," we help kids document their views of the world with writing, drawing, audio and video. Their stories are then captured and preserved in our digital Time Capsule.

Share

Before that year's time capsule is closed, kids learn about the contributions from other kids through a range of programming, e.g. exchanging books with their submissions, exploring work in the digital time capsule, and participating in a "pen pal" program.

Amplify

We collect significant amounts of qualitative data that gives us a window into how kids are doing and how they view the world. We will share this data with media, researchers, and policymakers to bring more attention to the perspectives shared by kids.

Why We Do It

There is a pressing youth mental health crisis in America;  more than 40 percent of teenagers state that they struggle with persistent feelings of sadness. Most interventions to address this crisis are reactive - helping to diagnose and treat kids who are struggling. Too little attention is paid to help kids develop the skills necessary to positively manage their emotions and experiences.

Yet, we know they are struggling.

The good news is that we have ways of helping. By supporting kids to process their experiences and emotions, and to learn from the experiences of others, we can support their healthy social-emotional development.

Serving Youth At a Critical Stage

We focus on youth ages 9-14 - the developmental stage when kids are becoming more independent, begin to see the point of others more clearly, and encounter a range of significant - and often challenging - emotional and social changes.
